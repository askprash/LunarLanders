Source: Overview of Guidance, Navigation and Control System of the TeamIndus Lunar Lander. Axiom Research Labs (TeamIndus), arXiv:1907.10955, 2019. https://arxiv.org/pdf/1907.10955
(Close paraphrase of the public source, for testing.)

GNC sensor selection shall be done to have redundancy only when the device does not have space heritage. Primary sensors are a single IMU and a single star sensor with flight heritage (no backup), plus a triad of analog sun sensors for contingency. For descent ranging, LIDAR, radar altimeters and laser rangefinders were considered; a pair of military-grade long-range laser rangefinders (LLRF) was chosen, with the second device serving as a backup (16 km range, +/-1 m). A cluster of short-range laser rangefinders (SLRF, +/-0.2 m) gives high-accuracy altitude during terminal descent; a pair of COTS cameras supports lateral-velocity estimation and hazard avoidance. The descent phases are Braking (IMU propagation augmented by LLRF range plus an onboard terrain model, with fuel-optimal uplinked profiles), Approach (closed-loop guidance), Turn, and Terminal Descent. In terminal descent a local terrain-relative-navigation frame is defined from the SLRF; vertical states come from a Kalman filter; lateral velocity comes from feature-matching on the camera images; a Safe Spot Identification function finds a hazard-free spot (up to 20 m divert) conditional on propellant availability. Feature-matching requires shadow-casting lighting, so an early-morning landing was chosen and the terrain database was prepared accordingly. Health checks for sensors are performed before the lunar descent; a failed LLRF is telecommanded out. No active fault detection and reconfiguration of the actuation system is planned during descent. Main-engine cutoff is at 2 m, RCS cutoff at 1 m, then freefall to touchdown.
