(Close paraphrase of the public source, for testing.)

The Morpheus Lander is a vertical takeoff and landing testbed demonstrating integrated GNC for autonomous landing and hazard avoidance. It uses a throttleable LOX/liquid-methane main engine with thrust vector control for body pitch and yaw, and fixed reaction-control thrusters for roll. The onboard GNC sensor suite includes a GPS receiver, a Space Integrated GPS/INS (SIGI), a Litton LN-200 IMU, and an Acuity laser altimeter. The primary payload, Autonomous Landing Hazard Avoidance Technology (ALHAT), scans and detects hazards such as boulders and craters at the targeted landing site and interfaces with the GNC to autonomously avoid landing on hazards. The vehicle is aerodynamically unstable, and mass, inertia, aerodynamics, and slosh vary greatly over the timeline, so an outer guidance loop interacts with an inner attitude-control loop. Control uses gain-scheduled PID/TVC for pitch and yaw and phase-plane autopilots for roll. Flex filters phase-stabilize slosh modes; slosh is also damped passively with tank baffles. Optimal guidance commands are generated for ascent, hover/descent, and landing. Robustness is verified with an ascent stability analysis tool across dispersed trajectories and with high-fidelity Monte Carlo simulation plus flight tests; design objectives require at least 3 dB and 20 degrees of gain and phase margin for the perturbed system.
