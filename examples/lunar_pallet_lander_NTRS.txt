Source: Guidance, Navigation, and Control for NASA Lunar Pallet Lander. Orphee, Hannan, Anzalone et al. (NASA MSFC), AAS GN&C 2019. NTRS 20190002101. https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/20190002101/downloads/20190002101.pdf
(Close paraphrase of the public source, for testing.)

The Lunar Pallet Lander performs powered descent with a Star-48BV solid rocket motor (SRM braking burn, thrust-vector controlled), then a liquid vertical-descent phase using twelve 100-lbf on/off descent thrusters and twelve 5-lbf attitude thrusters. Touchdown-position and velocity requirements (100 m precision landing, 3-sigma; max 2 m/s vertical and horizontal touchdown velocity) drive sensor selection. The navigation suite uses Terrain Relative Navigation (TRN), which correlates onboard descent imagery against stored lunar imagery to update lander position to within about 10 m, and a Navigation Doppler Lidar (NDL) altimeter/velocity sensor giving high-accuracy altitude and 3-axis velocity. A medium-grade LN-200S IMU, two star trackers, and six fine digital sun sensors provide attitude. Navigation runs a 6-state and a 12-state Kalman filter. Sensor scheduling by altitude: TRN starts about 9 km and ends about 500 m; NDL starts about 2 km and ends about 30 m; below about 30 m the lander navigates on the IMU only. Guidance uses a predictor-corrector SRM algorithm and a traded liquid-descent guidance. The concept explicitly notes Terrain Relative Navigation with no hazard avoidance. Control is PID/TVC on the SRM and phase-plane on/off pulsing for the liquid stage, with descent-engine off-pulsing for added control authority. The design was verified via a 200-case Monte Carlo in the GLASS Simulink simulation. This is a pre-PDR concept; the touchdown-detection sensor is TBD.
