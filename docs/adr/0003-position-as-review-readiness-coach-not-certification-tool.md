# Position as review-readiness coach, not certification tool

The product will produce readiness gaps, evidence requests, and cited red-team concerns rather than pass/fail certification claims. It can say what historical failure classes a ConOps resembles, what evidence a serious reviewer would expect at a startup readiness gate, and which questions should be closed before PDR/CDR-style decisions; it must not claim that a design is certified, compliant, flight-ready, or approved.

This matters because the project does not have mission-assurance authority, governing-program context, or enough domain-specific evidence to certify a space system. The useful role is to coach teams toward better human reviews by turning past lunar-landing failures into actionable evidence requests while keeping final judgment with qualified reviewers, customers, regulators, and mission assurance authorities.
