# Validate lunar corpus before adjacent corpora

The academic validation effort will remain lunar landing/descent focused first. Adjacent corpora such as Mars EDL, launch-vehicle GNC failures, aviation/autonomy safety cases, or terrestrial safety examples may become useful later, but they should not be mixed into the first validation set.

This keeps the initial research claim clean: a lunar-lander failure memory can surface recurring lunar landing failure classes and review-readiness questions. Adjacent sources should be tracked as a later expansion path once domain experts have evaluated whether the lunar-only CAST/STPA framing, source quality, hindcasts, and review-gate evidence model are credible.
